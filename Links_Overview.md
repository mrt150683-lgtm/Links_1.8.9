# Links — Your Personal Research Intelligence System

> *Capture everything. Connect anything. Understand it all—under your control.*

---

## What Is Links?

Links is a **privacy-first research tool** that helps you **collect, organise, and make sense of information**.... whether you're building a case, researching a topic, investigating something, or simply trying to connect the dots across a mountain of notes, documents, and web pages.

Think of it as a **secure research vault** that not only stores everything you throw at it, but quietly reads it, understands it, tags it, summarises it, and finds hidden connections between pieces of information. Your research database and files live on your computer, encrypted.

Unlike mainstream AI assistants, you have absolute control: which AI model analyzes your work (swap between providers anytime), what gets remembered (you decide, not the vendor), and how much data leaves your machine (from fully local to privacy-respecting providers that don't log).

**You own your research. You own your data. You own the choice.**

---

## What Can It Store?

Anything you come across during research:

- **Text snippets** — copy a paragraph, paste it in, Links remembers where it came from
- **Web pages & links** — save a URL with its title and an excerpt at the click of a button
- **Documents** — PDFs, Word files, reports — upload and store them securely
- **Images** — diagrams, screenshots, photos — all encrypted and searchable
- **Audio files** — recordings, interviews, voice memos — Links will transcribe them automatically
- **Chat conversations** — your own research conversations with the AI assistant are saved and searchable

Everything is stored in a **"Research Pot"** — an isolated, encrypted vault you create for a specific topic, case, or project. You can have as many pots as you like, and they stay completely separate from each other.

---

## What Does It Do Automatically?

Once you've saved something, Links goes to work in the background (when your computer is idle, so it never slows you down). It uses AI to:

- **Tags your content** — automatically applies labels like *person*, *location*, 
  
  *organisation*, *concept*, *event*

- **Extracts key entities** — pulls out names, organisations, dates, and places mentioned in the text

- **Summarises everything** — generates a concise, cited summary of each piece of content

- **Finds connections** — discovers when two pieces of information are related, and tells you *why*, with evidence and confidence scores

- **Transcribes audio** — turns voice recordings into searchable text and then processes them like any other document

- **Learns your style** — adapts how it talks to you based on your personal communication preferences over time

All AI-generated content is **clearly labelled as derived** — Links never confuses its own analysis with your original sources. You decide which AI model does the work (and can choose non-logging models or run everything locally on your GPU).

## The AI Research Assistant

Every Research Pot has a built-in **AI chat assistant** that has read everything inside that pot. It's powered by an AI model of your choice. You can:

- Ask questions about your research — *"What do these three documents have in common?"*
- Request summaries — *"Give me a brief overview of everything I've saved this week"*
- Explore connections — *"Are there any contradictions between these sources?"*
- Get analysis — *"What's still unanswered based on what I have?"*

The assistant can also run a **Deep Research mode** — a sophisticated autonomous agent that reads everything in your pot, generates a structured research plan, answers it methodically, and produces a full report with sources and open questions. You can schedule this to run automatically (daily, weekly, etc.) so you always have fresh insights waiting for you. Or configure it to run entirely on your local machine using open-source models.

---

## Discovery Engine (Scout & RepoForge)

Links includes a built-in **discovery engine** that helps you find real open-source projects related to your work.

- **Scout** — Search GitHub with smart filters (star range, language, activity) and get AI-scored results ranked by collaboration potential, novelty, and integration surface. High-scoring projects are automatically paired and you get a ready-made outreach brief with specific integration ideas.

- **RepoForge** — Point it at your own repository (or just a README from a project you're working on) and it figures out what searches to run, finds 2–4 complementary projects, and packages them into "Forge Packs" with step-by-step integration plans.

Both tools use a **two-model tiering** system — a cheap, fast model handles the bulk keyword and query work, while a premium model does the deep analysis and synergy reasoning. Every discovery is scored deterministically (not AI vibes) and every search, fetch, and analysis is fully auditable.

Nothing is automated beyond discovery and drafting — **you decide** what to act on and who to contact.

## Your Data, Your Privacy

- **Your database and files stay on your machine** — encrypted at rest, never stored in the cloud or in anyone's AI training data
- **You control which AI model does the work** — switch between OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepSeek, or any other provider supported by OpenRouter. Not locked into one company's ecosystem.
- **You choose what gets remembered** — unlike mainstream AI assistants, your conversation history is *yours* to manage. Links doesn't silently retain your chats. No vendor lock-in on your research history.
- **Pick models that don't log** — choose providers that explicitly don't retain or log your requests. Zero trace after analysis.
- **Or go completely local** — if you have a GPU, configure Links to use open-source models running directly on your machine. No data sent anywhere, ever.
- **Export bundles are encrypted** — if you share a pot with a colleague, it leaves your machine as a locked, tamper-proof package
- **No telemetry** — nothing is tracked, reported, or phoned home by Links itself
- **You own your audit trail** — everything is logged locally with full provenance (which model, which prompt version, when) so you can verify and reproduce any AI-generated insight

Even for personal use — save articles, voice notes, interesting links, and have a searchable, AI-assisted second brain that you actually own.

## A Day In The Life

> You're investigating a story. You save a dozen web pages using the browser extension — one right-click per page. You upload a PDF report you received by email. You record a voice note on your phone and drop the audio file in. You paste in a few paragraphs you copied from a paywalled article.
> 
> That evening, while your computer sits idle, Links reads everything. It transcribes your voice note, tags all your sources, extracts the names and organisations mentioned across all of them, and finds that three separate sources all reference the same company under slightly different names.
> 
> The next morning you open the chat, ask *"What connections exist between these sources?"* and get a clear, cited breakdown — with the exact sentences that led to each conclusion.
> 
> You then run a Deep Research session. Links reads your entire pot, generates 12 research sub-questions, answers each one from your material, and produces a structured report flagging two contradictions and four open questions that need more sources.
> 
> You export the pot as an encrypted file and send it to a colleague. They import it on their machine, everything intact, nothing leaked.

**Mainstream AI assistants:**

- Lock you into one company's ecosystem
- Retain your conversations forever in their systems
- Train on your data (even with "privacy" disclaimers)
- Control which models you can use
- You have no choice about what gets remembered

**Links:**

- Works with any AI provider you choose
- Your conversation history is yours alone — doesn't get sold to train other models
- Switch models or providers anytime — no lock-in
- You decide what gets remembered and what disappears
- Zero vendor control over your research history

This is the actual difference that matters: **you're not the product, and you're not trapped in anyone's walled garden.**

## Built to Last

Links is built with the same discipline as enterprise research software:

- Every piece of AI output is stored separately from your originals — your sources are never modified
- Everything is versioned and auditable — you can see exactly which AI model produced which output, with which instructions, at what time
- Connections are never invented — Links uses a two-stage process where AI can only classify relationships between pairs it has already identified as candidates, not invent new ones
- Evidence is always cited — summaries include exact text excerpts, not paraphrases
